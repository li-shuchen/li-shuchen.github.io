About Me

I am a second-year master's student in the Computer Science Department at Carnegie Mellon University. I am fortunate to be advised by Pravesh Kothari. I am broadly interested in theoretical computer science, particularly algorithms, computational complexity, and their intersection with mathematics. Recently, I have been thinking about problems in robust statistics (also working with Manolis Zampetakis) and in combinatorics.

Prior to CMU, I obtained my B.S. in computer science from the Turing Class at Peking University, where I worked with Qian Li on Boolean function analysis.
